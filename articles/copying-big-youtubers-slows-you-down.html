Mistakes

Why Copying Big YouTubers Slows You Down

Big creators can do things that are suicidal for small channels: long intros, broad topics, inside jokes. They already have momentum. You don’t. Copying them usually kills your traction.

Read time: 6 minutes Updated: 2025

Big channels have built-in trust

Their audience clicks because they already know the creator. You don’t have that advantage. Your packaging has to earn the click every time. That’s why CTR is so critical at the small stage.

Big channels can afford slower pacing

When you have an audience, viewers stick around. When you’re small, they bounce quickly. This is why I prioritize hooks and short intros. Watch time matters more than production flair.

Small channels need focus

Big creators can be broad. Small channels need a clear lane. If you’re still discovering yours, read Why Your First 100 Videos Probably Won’t Matter.

The fastest path is not copying big creators. It’s building a smaller, clearer promise you can actually deliver.
